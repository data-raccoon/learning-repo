# Case Assessment: Heinrich-Böll-Platz Guarding

## Identity and status

- **Review status:** `evidence_building`
- **Accountable analyst:** `data_analytics_engineer`
- **Decision owner:** To be confirmed across the City of Cologne, Veranstaltungszentrum Köln, and KölnMusik responsibilities.
- **Last evidence update:** 2026-07-17.
- **Publication status:** `internal_only`.

## Originating signal

- **Source and evidence role:** Bund der Steuerzahler NRW, `secondary_advocacy`.
- **Attributed claim:** Guarding and repeated closure of the plaza have generated persistent follow-on costs because activity on the plaza can disrupt the Philharmonie below.
- **Source date:** 2023-10-26; retrieval date 2026-07-17.
- **Why it warrants review:** The expenditure recurs over decades, affects public use of a prominent place, and appears linked to an unresolved physical or operating constraint.
- **Selection limitation:** The source selects conspicuous cases and uses advocacy language. Its conclusion is not adopted as a POC finding.

## Public purpose and baseline

- **Problem to be solved:** Prevent audible disturbance of rehearsals and performances in the Philharmonie while maintaining safety and lawful operation.
- **Intended beneficiaries:** Performers and audiences, the cultural institutions, plaza users, emergency services, and the wider public.
- **Mandate and constraints:** Safe Philharmonie operation, emergency access, the plaza's status as part of an artwork, and the interests of the artist or heirs are reported constraints. Exact legal and contractual duties remain unverified.
- **Original decision and lifecycle expectation:** Not yet reconstructed. The original building and plaza decision records and any lifecycle-cost appraisal are missing.
- **Historical financial baseline:** The City of Cologne's 2015 draft budget annex reports a 2015 plan of EUR 169,500 for guarding Heinrich-Böll-Platz, with lower reported plan or actual values in the preceding table columns.

## Current state

- **Operating condition:** According to the BdSt case, parts of the plaza are closed and guarded during rehearsals and performances because footsteps, wheeled luggage, and similar activity can be audible below.
- **Current financial evidence:** The official 2025 operating-plan explanation lists EUR 304,000 for guarding Heinrich-Böll-Platz. This is a plan value, not actual expenditure.
- **Nominal change:** EUR 304,000 is approximately 79 percent above the EUR 169,500 plan reported for 2015. This calculation is nominal and does not adjust for inflation, service hours, wage rates, procurement scope, or changed security requirements.
- **Recent actual evidence:** The 2024 financial statement reports EUR 286,667 for combined guarding of Heinrich-Böll-Platz and Flora, compared with EUR 295,165 in 2023. Because the line combines two sites, it is not directly comparable with the Böll-Platz-only plan value.
- **Resolution status:** No source reviewed establishes a funded permanent remedy or a current lifecycle comparison of alternatives.

## Cause and controllability analysis

- **Observed symptom:** Repeated guarding and restriction with a continuing annual cost.
- **Plausible causes:** Building and plaza acoustic interaction; artistic and heritage constraints; emergency-access requirements; lack of an affordable proven remedy; coordination with future Philharmonie refurbishment.
- **Alternative explanations:** Part of the cost may cover broader security or operations not separable from the acoustic issue.
- **What remains controllable:** Guarding specification and procurement, scheduling, current technical review, integration with refurbishment, and transparent lifecycle decision-making.
- **What is not established:** Negligence, avoidability at the original decision point, or the economic superiority of a particular remedy.

## Recommendation

### Preliminary assessment

The recurring follow-on cost and its material nominal increase are sufficiently established to warrant a formal lifecycle decision. The evidence does **not** establish that guarding is currently avoidable or that a capital intervention would have a positive net present value.

### Recommended action

Within a time-boxed internal review, assemble existing acoustic, safety, art-rights, operational, procurement, and refurbishment evidence into one lifecycle option dossier. Compare continued guarding, optimized operations, interim mitigation, and refurbishment-integrated remediation over a common horizon. Do not commission a new technical study until existing studies and decisions are indexed and their remaining gaps are explicit.

- **Expected effect:** A decision-ready basis to stop indefinite deferral or avoid an uneconomic capital response.
- **Financial effect:** Not yet quantifiable beyond the recurring plan line; use ranges and discounting in the option dossier.
- **Owner:** City function with budget and asset accountability, to be confirmed; KölnMusik and venue operations are required contributors.
- **Review point:** Founder review after primary technical and contract evidence is available.
- **Readiness:** Research recommendation ready; operational remedy not ready.
- **Confidence:** `medium-high` that a lifecycle review is justified; `low` regarding the preferred remedy.
