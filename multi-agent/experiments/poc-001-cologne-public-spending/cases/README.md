## Cross-case learning

- A recurring cost can be certain while the best intervention remains uncertain.
- Two official audit conclusions can substantiate a control deficiency without quantifying savings.
- An apparently successful policy change still needs actual post-change cost, demand, and access evidence.
- gpaNRW is materially relevant to the licence case but not automatically to a site-specific cultural-space or transit decision.
- INKAR is too aggregated for these three case-level decisions. That is a documented coverage result, not a reason to force unrelated indicators into the assessment.
- None of the cases currently supports a claim of misconduct or a citywide ranking.
- Live procurement creates a decision-timing signal but never proves poor value or authorizes interference.
- Operational and asset data are necessary to quantify realized savings and prioritize interventions; public reports provide a base layer, not a complete substitute.
