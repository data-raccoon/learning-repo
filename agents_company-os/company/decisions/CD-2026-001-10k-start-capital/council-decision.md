# Council Decision

## Decision ID

`CD-2026-001`

## Problem

The Founder must decide whether and how to allocate a maximum of EUR 10,000 of start capital while the portfolio has a supervised Company-OS and one discovery POC, but no approved Venture Charter, validated customer demand, paid-pilot evidence, or completed end-to-end data proof.

This decision is reversible at the planning level and becomes progressively less reversible when money, contracts, subscriptions, credentials, data, or external parties are involved. The proposed decision deadline is **2026-07-20**. Delay preserves cash but slows learning.

### Facts

- The planning ceiling is EUR 10,000; no allocation, spend, transfer, subscription, contract, credential change, outreach, data access, deployment, or publication is approved.
- The Cologne POC remains internal discovery. Its normalized three-product-group sample, semantic data-quality controls, and claim-level output traceability are incomplete.
- The Founder retains final authority over capital and external commitments. Silence is not approval.
- The five independent Council reports are complete: [Portfolio Strategy](portfolio-strategist.md), [Finance and Capital Allocation](finance-capital-allocator.md), [Legal, Risk and Governance](legal-risk-governance.md), [Venture Product](venture-product-lead.md), and [Technology Architecture](technology-architect.md).

### Inferences

- A product or platform commitment now would combine unresolved customer, evidence, usability, legal, and architecture risks.
- Small, evidence-gated releases should provide more decision-relevant learning and lower downside than a single large commitment.
- A 40% protected reserve is the most balanced Council position: it matches the Portfolio and Finance recommendations, is more conservative than Product's 30%, and is less conservative than Technology's 70% outside technology.

### Assumptions to resolve before any paid release

- The EUR 10,000 is available cash without a near-term repayment covenant.
- Its legal owner, funding characterization, entity, German registration, VAT, tax, bookkeeping, and reimbursement treatment will be documented.
- Founder labor is outside the cash envelope but will be tracked as an economic cost.
- Public or synthetic data and free or existing tools can support initial validation.
- One venture hypothesis will receive funded attention at a time unless the Founder separately approves an exception.

## Trade-offs

- **Reserve versus learning:** EUR 4,000 protects downside while allowing a maximum EUR 6,000 of separately gated activity. This is less conservative than Technology's portfolio reserve framing but more conservative than Product's EUR 3,000 reserve.
- **Customer evidence versus data evidence:** both are necessary. Customer validation prevents building for an interesting dataset; evidence-chain validation prevents testing a polished but unreliable proposition. Tranches 1 and 2 are separately gated and may overlap only after explicit Founder approval and confirmed owner capacity.
- **Technology exposure:** the consolidated plan assigns EUR 1,000 directly to the reusable evidence proof and caps all technical/tooling/hosting commitments across Tranches 2-4 at **EUR 2,000 gross**. This is below Technology's EUR 3,000 activation proposal and above Portfolio's EUR 1,250 shared-capability line because some product-test delivery may be technical. No broad platform, annual plan, hardware, production system, or public portal is in scope.
- **Control overhead versus speed:** finance, legal, privacy, security, accessibility, and transaction records add work, but prevent hidden liabilities and unusable evidence. Reviews should be proportional to the actual tranche risk.
- **Parallelism versus focus:** limited overlap of customer and evidence validation can shorten elapsed time, but funded venture hypotheses remain sequential by default and each path has one accountable owner.

### Release and no-spend boundary

1. **Decision now requested:** approve, reject, defer, or revise only the allocation framework.
2. **Not requested and not authorized:** any payment, transfer, reimbursement, vendor selection, subscription, contract or terms acceptance, credential/account creation or change, external communication, participant recruitment, incentive, controlled/personal-data access, deployment, or publication.
3. If the framework is approved, each tranche requires a separate Work Order and explicit Founder release. Material contracts, data, security, tax, production, and external-action gates remain independently applicable.
4. Finance records gross committed exposure, not only cash paid. Shortest practical terms, no uncontrolled auto-renewal, business-owned accounts, export/deletion paths, and complete closeout records are mandatory.
5. Freeze the affected tranche on suspected fraud, security/privacy incident, tax notice, dispute, missing records, approval breach, or forecast overrun. A reserve release or material reallocation requires a new Council decision.

### Downside and portfolio stop conditions

- If only T0 and T1 are fully used and evidence fails, maximum approved exposure would be EUR 2,000 and EUR 8,000 remains protected cash.
- If all non-reserve tranches including contingency are separately approved and fully used, maximum exposure is EUR 6,000 and EUR 4,000 remains protected.
- Stop or reframe if no Venture Charter passes intake; no named owner has capacity; customer evidence does not show a recurring material problem; the data proof cannot support reproducible claims; legal, privacy, security, accessibility, or procurement risk cannot be reduced within a tranche; or progress requires production build, annual commitments, hiring, debt, regulated activity, or reserve use.
