# Case Assessment: Lentpark Fixed-Route and TaxiBus Service

## Identity and status

- **Review status:** `evidence_building`
- **Accountable analyst:** `data_analytics_engineer`
- **Decision owner:** City of Cologne public-transport authority and KVB service planning, exact accountability to be confirmed.
- **Last evidence update:** 2026-07-17.
- **Publication status:** `internal_only`.

## Originating signal

- **Source and evidence role:** Bund der Steuerzahler 2018/19 follow-up, `secondary_advocacy`.
- **Attributed claim:** A poorly used extension of bus line 127 to Lentpark was replaced in December 2018 by a lower-cost demand-responsive TaxiBus.
- **Why it warrants review:** It offers a retrospective test of whether a service-model change reduced cost while preserving adequate access.
- **Selection limitation:** BdSt attributes the change partly to its advocacy and reports estimated rather than verified post-change cost.

## Public purpose and baseline

- **Purpose:** Provide proportionate public-transport access to Lentpark while connecting with the surrounding network and serving mobility needs.
- **Service baseline:** The extension reportedly operated from December 2014 to December 2018 with 21 daily trips.
- **Attributed pre-change cost:** BdSt reports EUR 180,000 per year, EUR 13,000 of provisional road works, and EUR 733,000 cumulative expenditure by December 2018.
- **Attributed demand:** BdSt reports approximately 10 boardings and 20 alightings per day in May 2017, down from approximately 20 and 40 respectively in autumn 2015.
- **Alternative:** A demand-responsive service existed in some form and the fixed-route extension was replaced by TaxiBus 184.

## Current state

- **Decision evidence:** Contemporary reporting records that the route extension was withdrawn because of low demand and TaxiBus service introduced from December 2018.
- **Current service evidence:** KVB and VRS sources show TaxiBus 184 serving Lentpark in 2026. The published timetable requires advance telephone booking and offers scheduled booking opportunities at 30-minute intervals over substantial daytime and evening periods.
- **Post-change actual cost:** Not located.
- **Post-change demand and access:** Calls, completed trips, denied requests, passenger characteristics, accessibility, wait time, and substitution to other modes were not located.
- **Realized savings:** Not established. The BdSt's approximately EUR 30,000 annual TaxiBus estimate depends on demand and is not an actual result.

## Cause and controllability analysis

- **Observed symptom:** Low reported demand for a fixed-route extension.
- **Plausible causes:** Existing nearby network, short additional access benefit, service design, awareness, schedule fit, destination demand, or measurement period.
- **Alternative explanation:** Low observed use may reflect poor service design rather than low underlying mobility need.
- **Controllable now:** Service format, booking channels, operating window, integration with other routes, measurement, and periodic review.
- **Not established:** The realized saving, adequate access for all users, or whether TaxiBus remains the best current model.

## Recommendation

### Preliminary assessment

The decision to replace the low-use fixed-route extension with demand-responsive service was plausibly efficiency-improving, but the POC cannot yet confirm realized savings or preserved effectiveness. Calling it a proven success would overstate the evidence.

### Recommended action

Complete a retrospective before-and-after evaluation using actual full cost and service evidence. At minimum compare annual operator cost, completed passenger journeys, cost per completed journey, booking attempts, rejected requests, reliability, accessibility, and alternative-network access. Use the result to define a reusable review trigger for low-volume scheduled and demand-responsive services.

- **Expected effect:** Validate or correct the claimed saving and turn one case into a repeatable service-review method.
- **Financial effect:** Unknown until actual TaxiBus cost and usage replace estimates.
- **Owner:** Public-transport authority with KVB service planning and accessibility review.
- **Review point:** After a minimum twelve-month recent TaxiBus dataset and historical cost reconciliation are available.
- **Readiness:** Outcome-evaluation recommendation ready; service redesign not ready.
- **Confidence:** `high` that evaluation is justified; `medium` that the 2018 change reduced cost; `low` that effectiveness was preserved.
